Xavier's Industry Data Reflection:

The specific skills for my required skill set in terms of being an ICT support officer were far more frequent in the higher end of employment requirements. Overall, they were skills around technical support and social interactions. There were not an extensive number of skills required but they were predominantly skills that are in higher demand. However, there were still many skills that were missing from the requirements, and most demanded of those were skills in Java and JavaScript. There was also not a requirement to have SQL. These are all programming languages as ICT support does not inherently require knowledge of specific coding languages, unless outlined by the company employing their ICT support team. Business management was the most required skill that was non-coding based and whilst team management would be a part of an ICT support officers role, managing a business is less crucial and they are the technical support for an already existing business with management already designated to run the company.

Liam’s Industry Data Reflection

The requirements of an IT Operations Manager seem to be placed evenly from high demand to low. This is probably due to the fact that this roll is often a final elevation point before requiring external intervention. This roll also requires to oversee this progress of many other positions in technical roles, so an understanding of their work type is also required. The role of an IT Operations Manager requires a wide variety of non-technical skills as well as this role sits between those who perform technical roles such as service desk workers or developers and those in executive positions or general staff. While also managing, planning and adhering to SLA’s of the team.
The three IT-specific skills that are not a major component of an IT Operations Managers role are SQL, JavaScript and JAVA as these are highly technical skills where-as this role requires more management skills. Three general skills that are not required of this role are Writing, Creativity and Articulation. While any role anywhere would require a minimum understanding of these, they are not a major component of this role. This information has not changed my willingness to peruse this role, this is mainly because I was aware of the scope of this role by watching my operations manager perform his role. While this role might not be in the highest demand this is a role that incorporates both technical skills and people skill, which is something that I want.

Maxim’s Industry Data Reflection

Many of the most crucial IT specific skills for my ideal job as a cyber security specialist were also in highest demand from employers. More specifically, out of the top 30 of the most in demand IT specific skills from required from employers, 14 of these are necessary in my role. This is due to the fact that I will require an in-depth knowledge of software and hardware principles, and any way this knowledge can be used to breach security measures of an organization. Over half of the general skills in demand from employers will be necessary in my role. This demonstrates the necessity for me to not only have a well-rounded grasp of the IT industry, but to also be able to put these skills in practice effectively in a team environment. The abilities to articulate and plan new security measures, with a meticulous level of attention to detail will be crucial as stakes are particularly high.
The three highest ranked IT-specific skills which are NOT in my required skills set are Business Analysis, Business Management, and graphic design. These skills will not be required heavily in my role in cyber security as my role is highly technical and less business administration oriented. Graphic design will not be required unless I need to present PowerPoints or share data, which is not one of the most crucial aspects of my role. The three most highly ranked general skills not required in my role are organization skills, creativity and leadership skills. This may change in the future as my career progresses; however they will be less necessary in the beginning. After looking further into the Burning Glass data, I have more of an appreciation for how much knowledge I will need to obtain in the coming years and I am excited continue on this path.
