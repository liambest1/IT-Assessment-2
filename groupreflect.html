Group Reflection

Xavier’s Reflection

Overall, I think the organisation of the group and the schedule the group made to stay on task worked well. Our group leader made a huge effort to get everyone organised quickly at the start and communicated with the rest of the group that got us to come to an agreement to make a schedule to meet up. We also worked well at dividing the workload into mostly equal parts and making sure people were comfortable with the work that was given to them. We worked together well to get everything done by agreed upon deadlines.
An improvement to be made was our communication towards the start of the project. Whilst this was solved in the later weeks, early on we were very unsure about each other’s timetables and this led to a lot of concern around whether certain members of the group were understanding how we planned on proceeding with the assignment.
Something that surprised me was the willingness of every participant in our group to find motivation, and keep communicating with one another, without a single person taking over the project. Whilst we had an overall group manager, it never felt as though they were completely in charge and instead, everyone’s contributions were validated by the group.
I learned that group work is easy provided that the group keeps communication and as an introverted person there was a lot of hesitation from me, about working in a group. However, if people maintain communication, the group runs in a comfortable environment.

Group Reflection

What went well

Communication has been a strong point for Team Exodus. We had regular meetings with 2 meetings each week, often between 45 minutes and 1 hour in length. We kept very active in the Microsoft Teams chat as well, with all 6 members often communication at once every day. Delegation of tasks was another area of strength, with each member always having a clear view of the tasks the were required to contribute towards.
Our Microsoft Word document now has over 320 revisions, which shows how active we have been as a group. This is much more reflective of our group's efforts than our GitHub repository.

What could be improved

There could be an improvement regarding chairing the meeting and minute taking. The way we originally designated these roles was to have a team member alternate each meeting to do both tasks. Going forward a change we could make to improve this would be dividing these roles between two people so that it is easier for the person chairing the meeting to organise and run the session without needing to split their focus between running the meeting and recording minutes.

At least one thing that was surprising

One thing was surprising was the ease in which in member was able to work together with the rest of the team, considering that members are in different class group in this subject. This may have also been an advantage as members were attending lectures from (3?) different teachers each week. This helped our team members attain more knowledge from the lecturers and also pick up tips on how to get the best marks.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups

Communication was a big factor in what we learned as a group, and many of us agreed in our reflection on group work that with good communication we were able to work effectively as a group and communication around dividing up the work made the group efforts run smoothly, playing into our strengths. Many of us were apprehensive about group work however we are able to communicate effectively to get organised and get our work done with efficiency.

Summary

As a group we have a lot of different personality types showed in our personality tests. This allowed the entire group, who have different strengths and weaknesses to come together and support one another where we may have had difficulties. Our strengths allowed us to excel in the areas where we performed the best. Our different knowledge and experiences were excellent assets to our group project.
